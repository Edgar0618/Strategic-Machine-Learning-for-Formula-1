Tire Strategy Insights

Soft Tires

Best for qualifying and short stints. Optimal for 15-20 laps in dry conditions.

Medium Tires

Balanced performance. Ideal for 25-30 laps, good for race start and middle stint.

Hard Tires

Longest lasting. Best for 35+ laps, optimal for one-stop strategies.

Intermediate Tires

Wet conditions. Critical for mixed weather, can gain 2-3 positions in wet conditions.

Weather Strategy Insights

Wet Weather Strategy

Intermediate tires are crucial for wet conditions. Early pit stops for tire changes can gain significant positions.

Dry Weather Strategy

One-stop strategies are optimal in dry conditions. Tire management becomes the key differentiator.

Mixed Conditions

Flexibility in strategy is crucial. Teams that adapt quickly to changing conditions gain the most positions.

Model Development

Linear Regression

  • Used scikit-learn's LinearRegression
  • Train-test split: 80/20
  • Features: Qualifying position
  • Target: Position change

Gradient Boosting

  • Used GradientBoostingRegressor
  • Random state: 42 for reproducibility
  • Features: Driver, track, tire type, strategy
  • Target: Race placement

Random Forest

  • Used RandomForestRegressor
  • 100 estimators
  • Features: Driver, tire, weather, pit stops
  • Target: Race time

Evaluation Metrics
